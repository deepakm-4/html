DRUG ADDICTION


Drug addiction is a serious and growing problem that affects millions of people worldwide. It is a condition where individuals become dependent on substances such as Cocaine, Marijuana(Cannabis), Methanpetamine or other illegal drugs. Over time, the body and mind develop a tolerance, leading the person to use larger amounts to achieve the same effect. This dependency can severely impact a person’s physical and mental health, relationships, and overall quality of life.
The causes of drug addiction vary, but often include genetic factors, environmental influences, and emotional or psychological issues . Peer pressure, stress, and trauma can also contribute to the initiation of drug use. Once a person becomes addicted, it becomes increasingly difficult to break free, as drugs alter the brain’s chemistry, making it hard to feel pleasure without them.
In conclusion, drug addiction is a complex and destructive issue, but with proper support and treatment, individuals can overcome it and rebuild their lives.
